Welcome. My website explores the intersection of English, Swiss, and European transport law – and lots more.

Nice to see you here!

I’m a law student at the University of Liverpool. While many of my peers are focused on the courtroom, I’ve found my home in the library, diving into the theoretical side of maritime and railway law. I’m fascinated by how English and Swiss legal frameworks compare, and I spend most of my academic time exploring the ‘why’ behind international transport regulations.

When I’m not researching, I stay busy as a Parish Councillor and a Treasurer for our university’s legal societies – roles that give me a front-row seat to how law works in the real world. I’m a big fan of analytical rigour (thanks to plenty of mooting and debating) and I’m currently shaping my path toward a career in academia. I’m a native English speaker, also fluent in German (B2), and I’d love to connect with anyone interested in legal theory or cross-border research!
